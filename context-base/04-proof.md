# Proof

> Purpose: the evidence that makes every claim credible. The AI should never
> assert a result it can't ground in something here. Specificity is what
> separates a believable claim from marketing noise — real numbers, real names,
> real artifacts. This doc grows every week as you generate more results.

> **Honesty rule:** only put real, defensible facts here. If you wouldn't say it
> to the buyer's face with a screenshot ready, it doesn't belong. The AI is more
> useful when it's less likely to invent things — don't undermine that by
> feeding it fiction.

## Testimonials & quotes

> FILL IN: Verbatim quotes with attribution (name, title, company). Even short

## Numbers I can stand behind

> FILL IN: Aggregate stats — clients served, average result, total value
> delivered, retention/repeat rate. Only ones you can defend.

## Credibility markers

> FILL IN: Logos, notable clients, certifications, relevant background, press,
> partnerships, audience size. Anything that borrows trust legitimately.

## Proof gaps (be honest)

> FILL IN: Where you're thin on proof right now, and the plan to fill it (e.g.
> "no named logo yet → offer a discounted pilot in exchange for a case study").
> Naming the gap keeps the AI from overclaiming to cover it.
