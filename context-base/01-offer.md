# Offer

> Purpose: the single source of truth for what you sell. Everything the AI
> pitches, references, or builds toward comes from here. If this doc is vague,
> every downstream message is vague.

---

## The one-liner

> FILL IN: One sentence. "I help [specific buyer] [achieve specific outcome]
> in [timeframe] without [the thing they dread]." No adjectives doing the work
> of nouns.

## The problem I solve

> FILL IN: The specific, expensive problem. Name what it costs them — in money,
> time, risk, or missed revenue — when it goes unsolved. Urgency closes; vague
> nice-to-haves don't. If the problem isn't painful enough to fund, the whole
> machine sends thousands of messages and books nothing.

## Who it's for (summary)

> FILL IN: One or two lines naming the buyer. Full detail lives in `02-icp.md`.

## The outcome

> FILL IN: The concrete after-state. Prefer a measurable delta ("from X to Y")
> over a feeling ("peace of mind").

## What's actually delivered

> FILL IN: The scope. Deliverables, cadence, what's included, what's explicitly
> not. Ambiguity here becomes scope creep and refund requests later.

## Price & structure

> FILL IN: The model and the number. Be honest about which path to $1M this
> offer is on:
> - Retainer at ~$5k/mo → ~17 clients clears $1M/yr (~$83k/mo).
> - One-time projects at $20k–$50k → far fewer deals, but lumpier revenue.
> State the actual price. "It depends" is not a price.

## Risk reversal

> FILL IN: Guarantee, pilot, or proof-of-concept that lowers the buyer's
> perceived risk of saying yes. What makes "no" feel more expensive than "yes"?

## Why now

> FILL IN: Why this is urgent for the buyer *this quarter*, not someday. Tie to
> a trigger if you can (new funding, a hire, a deadline, a cost bleeding now).

---

## EXAMPLE (fictional — for quality bar only; delete when filling in)

**One-liner:** I help Series-A B2B SaaS companies cut new-customer onboarding
time from 6 weeks to under 10 days, so revenue activates before the customer
churns — without adding headcount to their CS team.

**Problem I solve:** Post-raise SaaS teams onboard customers manually because
nobody owned the process during the scramble to ship. Onboarding drags 4–8
weeks, customers stall before first value, and 20–30% churn inside 90 days.
At a $40k ACV, every 10 stalled accounts is ~$400k of ARR bleeding out while
the CS team firefights instead of scaling.

**Outcome:** Time-to-first-value from ~40 days to <10. Measured against their
own activation metric, reported weekly.

**Delivered:** A 6-week engagement — onboarding audit, a rebuilt activation
flow, in-app guides, and a CS playbook handoff. Includes 2 weeks of post-launch
tuning. Does not include building net-new product features.

**Price:** $28k fixed-fee project. (Path: ~25–30 projects/yr → ~$700k–$840k,
plus retainer upsells to clear $1M.)

**Risk reversal:** Activation-metric audit in week 1; if we can't model a
credible path to halving time-to-value, they don't proceed and pay only the
audit fee.

**Why now:** They just raised and the board is watching net revenue retention.
Churn at 90 days is the number that kills the Series B story.
