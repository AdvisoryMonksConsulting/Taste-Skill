# Positioning

> Purpose: why you, and not the alternatives. This is what the AI reaches for
> when it needs to differentiate, handle a "why should I care / why you" moment,
> or frame the offer against what the buyer is doing today. Positioning is
> relative — it only means something next to the options the buyer is weighing.

---

## The category I'm in (and the one I'm against)

> FILL IN: What shelf does the buyer mentally file you on? Sometimes the sharper
> move is to reject the obvious category. ("Not another onboarding consultant —
> a fixed-scope activation sprint.")

## The alternatives the buyer is actually choosing between

> FILL IN: Be honest and concrete. Usually includes:
> - **Do nothing / status quo** (the real default competitor — beat this first)
> - **DIY / hire internally**
> - **A named competitor or category of competitor**
> For each, name what it costs them or where it falls short for *this* buyer.

## What makes me genuinely different

> FILL IN: 2–4 points that are true, specific, and hard to copy. Not "quality"
> or "we care" — those are table stakes. A real mechanism, a unique method, a
> proof asset, a focus nobody else has. If a competitor could write the same
> sentence, cut it.

## My unique mechanism

> FILL IN: The named "how." Buyers trust a process with a name and a shape more
> than a promise. What's the repeatable method that produces your outcome?

## Objections & honest answers

> FILL IN: The 3–5 real objections, each with a straight answer. The AI uses
> these in follow-ups and replies. Don't dodge — a credible answer to "you're
> expensive" builds more trust than pretending the objection doesn't exist.

## What I am NOT / who I'm not for

> FILL IN: Stating the boundary sharpens the position. Saying who you're wrong
> for makes the right buyer trust that you're right for them.

## EXAMPLE (fictional — delete when filling in)

**Category:** Not an onboarding consultancy and not a CS-ops hire — a
fixed-scope, 6-week activation sprint with a metric attached.

**Alternatives:**
- *Status quo:* keep firefighting; lose 20–30% to 90-day churn. Cost: ~$400k
  ARR per cohort, and a board that stops trusting the NRR forecast.
- *Hire a CS-ops lead:* $140k+/yr, 3 months to ramp, and they still have to
  invent the playbook from scratch.
- *Big-name CX consultancy:* $80k+, 4-month engagement, deck-heavy, no
  in-product execution.

**Genuinely different:**
1. Tied to *their* activation metric, reported weekly — outcome, not output.
2. In-product execution (we build the flow), not a slide deck of advice.
3. Fixed 6 weeks, fixed fee — no open-ended retainer creep.

**Unique mechanism:** The "First-10-Days Activation Map" — we instrument the
real path to first value, find the three biggest drop-off points, and rebuild
just those. Narrow by design.

**Objections:**
| Objection | Honest answer |
|-----------|---------------|
| "$28k is a lot." | It's <1 month of a CS-ops hire and recovers one churned account. |
| "We can do this internally." | You can — but it's been on the roadmap 8 months. The sprint is the forcing function. |
| "6 weeks is too fast." | We rebuild three drop-off points, not the whole product. Scope is the point. |

**Not for:** PLG/self-serve products, pre-revenue startups, or teams wanting a
permanent embedded resource.
